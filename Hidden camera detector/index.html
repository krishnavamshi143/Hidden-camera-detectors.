Countermeasures & Safety Tips

1. Two-Way Mirror Test

Place your fingernail against the mirror. If there is a gap between your nail and the reflection, it is a regular mirror. If your nail touches its reflection directly (no gap), it may be a two-way mirror. Verify by shining a bright light through it.

2. Physical Coverage

If you suspect an object (e.g., clocks, smoke detectors, decorative vases) contains a camera but cannot locate it, cover the suspicious lens opening with opaque tape or drape a cloth over the item.

3. Low-Light Glint Searching

Turn off all room lights, turn on your phone's flashlight, and look through the camera with the Glint filter. Camera lenses have highly reflective glass surfaces that act as retro-reflectors, flashing bright pinpoints back to you.
